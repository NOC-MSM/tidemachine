Simulation of Kelvin's Tide Predicting Machine

The functioning of Kelvin's tide predicting mechanism, and the way in which the various constituents contribute to the tide. In the simulated mechanism nine constituents are summed: M2, S2, N2, K1, O1, P1, K2, M4 and M6. The initial phases are all set to zero (upper pulleys) or 180o (lower). The amplitudes can be selected from the pull-down menu to match those from one of seven ports.

Click to activate. Horizontal lines on the graph are spaced 1m apart. The vertical bars mark off 24-hour days.

In this simulation the constituents all start out in phase; the typical aspect of the tidal record for the port does not appear until after a simulated week or two.
